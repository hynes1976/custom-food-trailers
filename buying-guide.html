How to Choose a Food Trailer

Ready to start your food business? Protect your investment with this complete guide to buying a food trailer in the UK.

Buying a food trailer is one of the biggest decisions you will make when launching or growing a mobile food business. Get it right and you have a reliable, legal, brand defining asset that pays for itself. This guide walks through the key choices, size, budget, power, compliance and towing, so you order with confidence.

1. Start with your menu and covers

Everything flows from what you sell and how many customers you serve at peak. A coffee and pastry operation needs very different worktop, water and refrigeration to a loaded fries or burger kitchen. List your equipment first, then work out the counter space and power it demands. That tells you the size before you fall in love with a look.

2. Choose the right size

Our range spans 3m to 7m. As a rough guide:

  • 3m to 4m: coffee, crepes, single operator street food. Easy to tow and quick to pitch.
  • 4.5m to 5m: the sweet spot for busy service and twin operator setups.
  • 6m to 7m: high volume events, bars and full menus that need maximum storage and frontage.

Bigger is not always better: a larger trailer costs more, is heavier to tow and can be harder to pitch. Buy the size your menu genuinely needs, with a little headroom to grow.

3. Set a realistic budget

Custom trailers start around £14,950 and rise with size and specification. Budget for the build plus the options that matter to your operation, custom paint or wrap, extra refrigeration, an extended canopy and your power setup. Factor in running costs too: gas, insurance, pitch fees and servicing.

4. Plan your power

How you power the trailer shapes where you can trade. Mains hook up is simplest where it is available. For off grid pitches, a lithium battery bank with an inverter, optional solar and a quiet generator such as the Honda EU22i gives flexibility. Size the system to your equipment load so you never run short mid service.

5. Insist on compliance and quality

A professional trailer should be built to UK and EU standards with certified electrics, an AL-KO chassis, brakes and stability jacks, and a VIN number. Look for double skin insulation, stainless steel counters and an aluminium non slip floor, the details that stand up to daily commercial use and to inspection.

6. Check towing and logistics

Confirm your driving licence entitlement and your towing vehicle's capacity against the trailer's gross weight. Think about how you will manoeuvre and level it on your regular pitches, and where you will store and service it between trading days.

7. Buy from a builder who supports you

The best trailers come with a relationship, not just a receipt. Choose a maker who helps you specify the right build, keeps you updated during fabrication and is there afterwards with warranty and advice. That partnership protects your investment long after collection day.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a food trailer cost in the UK?

Custom built food trailers typically start around £14,950 for a compact 3m trailer and rise to around £21,950 for a 7m build, before options such as power upgrades, custom paint and extra equipment.

What size food trailer do I need?

Match size to your menu and expected covers. Compact 3m to 4m trailers suit coffee and single operator street food, 4.5m to 5m suits busy twin operator service, and 6m to 7m suits high volume events and full kitchens.

Do I need a special licence to tow a food trailer?

It depends on your driving licence category and the trailer's gross weight. Drivers who passed their test after 1 January 1997 should check their entitlement, and heavier trailers may require category BE. Always confirm your towing vehicle's capacity.

How is a food trailer powered off grid?

Options include lithium battery banks with inverters, solar top up and a quiet generator such as the Honda EU22i. The right setup depends on your equipment load and how long you trade between charges.
